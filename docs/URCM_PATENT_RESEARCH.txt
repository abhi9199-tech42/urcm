Objective
- Conduct focused prior art research relevant to URCM (resonance-based cognition, phoneme→frequency mapping, μ-convergence telemetry, typed A* with numeric envelopes) and compare to transformer/self‑attention inventions; propose patentability and FTO guidance.

URCM Technical Focus
- Compact phoneme set mapped to continuous resonance frequency vectors for reasoning, not just speech output.
- μ-convergence telemetry with explicit ρ/χ metrics and gated refusal policy on numeric/ontological infeasibility.
- Typed A* planning with extended envelopes (bilinear, square, chain, log, sigmoid, piecewise), integer enforcement, adaptive segmentation.
- CPU-first deterministic inference, caching of phoneme access, bounded latent memory footprint, compression ratios tied to μ.

Observations and Differentiators
- URCM does not perform token embedding or multi‑head attention; it employs resonance frequency vectors derived from phoneme mapping for reasoning. This sidesteps transformer prior art that centers on attention mechanisms over discrete tokens.
- URCM telemetry (μ=ρ/(1+χ)) and convergence labeling integrated with numeric feasibility envelopes and typed A* planning is distinct from TTS/ASR phoneme patents, which focus on audio synthesis or recognition, not a general reasoning engine or numeric solver gating.
- Oscillatory neural patents often claim hardware oscillator arrays or memristive circuits; URCM’s resonance is computational/representational (software) with envelope‑solver integration, reducing overlap with hardware claims while preserving a novel pipeline.
- Caching in URCM concerns frequency access and bounded latent state for reasoning; speech synthesis caching claims focus on join costs in unit‑selection synthesis, with different purpose and data structures.

Patentability Assessment (Preliminary)
- Likely claimable areas
  - Method/system for resonance‑based reasoning using compact phoneme→frequency mapping, with μ‑convergence telemetry gating and refusal policy enforcing typed/ontological and numeric feasibility constraints.
  - Envelope‑integrated typed A* planning with integer enforcement and adaptive segmentation, coupled to μ telemetry to drive convergence thresholds and control flow.
  - CPU‑first deterministic pipeline achieving bounded latent memory and measured compression ratios, with explicit telemetry APIs for ρ/χ/μ and convergence labeling.
- Risk areas (need claim drafting care)
  - Broad claims on “resonance neural networks” can run into oscillator hardware prior art; keep claims to software frequency‑vector representations, numeric envelopes, and planning integration.
  - Phoneme mapping is widely patented in TTS/ASR; distinguish purpose (reasoning, numeric feasibility), data forms (latent vectors, envelopes), and telemetry‑driven gating.
  - Avoid claiming general “attention” or “token embeddings”; focus on non‑token continuous resonance vectors and typed constraints.

Freedom‑to‑Operate (FTO) Considerations
- Transformer attention patents (US10452978B2, US11556786B2) are avoided if URCM does not use self‑attention over tokens; maintain architecture separation and avoid attention‑like modules.
- Hardware oscillator/memristor reservoir claims (US11164068B1, US9165246B2) do not apply to pure software models; FTO is stronger if no specialized hardware or oscillator arrays are used.
- TTS/ASR phoneme patents: ensure URCM does not generate audio output using claimed synthesis pipelines; the mapping to resonance vectors for cognition and numeric planning is a distinct use case.
- If any audio synthesis module is added later, review WO2004025626A1, EP0582377A2, US7983919B2 for overlap and design around.

Draft Claim Skeleton (Illustrative, non‑legal)
- Independent method claim
  - Receiving text and mapping tokens/characters to a compact phoneme set
  - Generating continuous resonance frequency vectors from phonemes
  - Executing typed A* planning with extended numeric envelopes and integer enforcement to produce feasible states
  - Computing ρ, χ and μ=ρ/(1+χ); gating transitions based on μ thresholds and labeling convergence when Δμ<ε
  - Emitting results and telemetry, including refusal events on numeric or ontological infeasibility
- System claim and non‑transitory medium claim mirroring the method
- Dependent claims for: adaptive envelope segmentation; cache policies for frequency access; telemetry APIs; ontology domain/range type enforcement; compression ratio measurement tied to μ
